CHAIRMAN'S STATEMENT


Freddie Davis-English

An African proverb advises us, "The ruin of a nation begins in the homes of its people." Conversely, the opposite is also true: homes filled with happiness, health and hope form the cornerstone of any thriving community. Children raised in this positive environment possess unshakable self-efficacy, and are rightfully convinced of their ability to contribute, overcome, create, and succeed.

At African American Family Services, our clients we are like everyone: they desperately want, and deserve, nurturing and empowering homes. They want this for their children. But for many, issues of chemical dependency, joblessness, gang involvement, prison, poverty, and violence have become a way of life. Generations of oppression and prejudice have altered the way once-proud families view and interact with their world, chipping away at hope and perpetuating negativity.

However, we believe in human resilience. We believe in change. Thus, our work with parents employs the same best practices we use to change behaviors, hearts and minds at all levels. We offer education to improve critical thinking and decision-making. We develop opportunities to foster self-reliance and confidence. Most importantly, we expose parents to new ways of behaving, believing and living, helping them improve their overall quality of life and perhaps even inspiring them to embrace this fact: all families possess value, integrity, and meaning. Their family counts.

With our focus on sustained behavior change and the continued development of positive support systems, we have an opportunity to create change for future generations. Just as maladaptations develop over time to weaken family systems, the seeds of strength can be planted, tended, and grown to produce positivity, peace and pride. Parents can change their lives, offering a better tomorrow for their children, themselves, and their communities.

It is my sincerest belief that African American Family Services will be a leader in helping our people achieve all that they want.
